This is the Deferred Production Today KPI card on the Fleet Overview.

What it means:

The platform is calculating that 2,124 barrels per day of oil production is being lost right now across the fleet, worth approximately $149,000/day in revenue.

How it's calculated:

For each asset (pumps and separators that have flow data):


Deferred = (Design Flow Rate − Actual Flow Rate) × 24 hours
Design Flow Rate comes from ASSET_FINANCIALS.DESIGN_FLOW_RATE — the rated capacity we populated (e.g. P-101 is rated at 165 m³/h)
Actual Flow Rate comes from the latest FLOW_RATE sensor reading from PROCESS_TRENDS
If actual flow < design flow, the gap is "deferred" production
Revenue = deferred barrels × $72.50/bbl (from COMMODITY_PRICE_PER_BBL)
Example: If P-101 is rated at 165 m³/h but currently running at 120 m³/h, that's 45 m³/h × 24h = 1,080 m³ of lost throughput, converted to barrels.

Why it's red: Any deferred production is flagged red because it represents direct revenue loss tied to equipment underperformance — typically caused by assets running degraded (low health scores, fouling, pump wear, etc.). It directly connects asset health to business impact, which is the core purpose of the platform.
